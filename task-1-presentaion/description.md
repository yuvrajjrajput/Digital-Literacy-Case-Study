# Task 1 – Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

## Tool Used
[Canva](canva.com)

## What the Infographic Shows

The infographic is titled **"Digital Literacy – Your Guide to the Digital World"** and
covers three key topics:

1. **What is Digital Literacy?**
   - The ability to use digital tools safely, responsibly, and effectively.
   - Skills include: finding information, evaluating sources, communicating online,
     and staying safe.

2. **Useful Digital Tools for Students**
   - Google Docs – writing and collaborating on assignments
   - Canva – creating presentations and infographics
   - GitHub – storing and sharing code projects
   - Google Forms – building surveys and quizzes

3. **Safe Internet Practices**
   - Use strong, unique passwords for every account
   - Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
   - Never click on suspicious links or emails
   - Verify a website is secure (https://) before entering any details
   - Do not share personal information publicly online

## Reflection

For Task 1, I used Canva to create a one-page Digital Literacy infographic. Canva is
a free browser-based design tool that provides a wide range of ready-made templates,
making it easy to get started even without any prior design experience. I searched for
"education infographic" templates and selected a colourful, multi-section layout that
was suitable for presenting information in a clear, visual format.

My infographic covers three key topics — what Digital Literacy means, useful digital
tools for students such as GitHub and Google Docs, and safe internet practices including
using strong passwords and avoiding suspicious links. I used icons, colour-coded
sections, and short bullet points to make the information easy to read at a glance.

One thing I found interesting was how many free templates Canva offers — it was
initially difficult to choose just one. A challenge I faced was fitting all the content
neatly into a single page without it looking cluttered. I resolved this by shortening
my sentences and replacing long paragraphs with bullet points and simple icons.
Overall, this task helped me understand how visual communication can make complex
information more accessible.
