# Stay Safe Online – Cybercrime Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

---

## General Online Safety

- [ ] **Always check the URL** before entering any login details. Look for `https://`
      and verify the domain is correct (e.g., `vitbhopal.ac.in` not `vitbhopal-portal.info`).

- [ ] **Never click links in unsolicited emails or SMS messages.** Instead, open your
      browser and type the official website address manually.

- [ ] **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. A strong password has at
      least 8 characters and includes uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols.

- [ ] **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on your email, college portal,
      and social media accounts. This adds a second layer of security even if your
      password is stolen.

- [ ] **Do not use public Wi-Fi** (cafes, malls, railway stations) to access your
      bank account, college portal, or any site that requires a password.

- [ ] **Log out of shared computers** after use in libraries, labs, or cyber cafes.
      Never save passwords on shared devices.

- [ ] **Keep your phone and apps updated.** Software updates often contain security
      patches that fix known vulnerabilities.

---

## Financial & UPI Safety (Specific to India)

- [ ] **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone** — not even someone
      claiming to be from your bank, PhonePe, GPay, or Paytm support. Legitimate
      companies will NEVER ask for these details.

- [ ] **Be cautious of "collect requests" on UPI apps.** Scammers send fake collect
      requests pretending you need to approve a payment to *receive* money. Approving
      it will debit your account, not credit it.

- [ ] **Only use official UPI apps** downloaded from the Google Play Store or Apple
      App Store. Avoid third-party apps that offer UPI transfers.

- [ ] **Verify the receiver's UPI ID carefully** before making any payment. Once a
      UPI transaction is confirmed, it cannot be reversed.

---

## If You Are Targeted

- [ ] **Do not panic.** Immediately change your passwords for any accounts that
      may have been compromised.

- [ ] **Report the incident** to your college IT department and/or cybercrime authorities.

- [ ] **Block the sender** on email/WhatsApp and report the number/address as spam.

---

## Reporting Channels

| Channel | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** (24x7, toll-free) |
| **College IT Support** | Contact your institution's IT helpdesk |

> If you have lost money due to online fraud, call **1930 immediately** — early
> reporting increases the chances of recovering your funds.
