# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

---

## DO's ✅

| # | Do This |
|---|---------|
| 1 | **Keep your profile professional** – Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a bio that reflects your academic identity. |
| 2 | **Think before you post** – Ask yourself: would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this? |
| 3 | **Use strong, unique passwords** – Use a different password for each platform and enable two-factor authentication (2FA). |
| 4 | **Verify news before sharing** – Check if a news story is from a credible source before reposting or forwarding it. |
| 5 | **Report harmful content** – If you see cyberbullying, hate speech, or fake news, use the platform's report feature. |
| 6 | **Keep your privacy settings updated** – Regularly review who can see your posts, location, and personal details. |
| 7 | **Give credit when sharing others' work** – Always tag or credit the original creator when sharing photos, videos, or articles. |

---

## DON'Ts ❌

| # | Avoid This |
|---|-----------|
| 1 | **Don't share your location publicly** – Avoid posting your home address, college hostel block, or real-time location. |
| 2 | **Don't post offensive or hateful content** – Comments that target any group based on religion, gender, or race can have serious consequences. |
| 3 | **Don't use the same password everywhere** – If one account is hacked, all your accounts become vulnerable. |
| 4 | **Don't engage with trolls or abusers** – Responding escalates the situation. Block, report, and move on. |
| 5 | **Don't share unverified information** – Spreading misinformation, even accidentally, can harm others and damage your credibility. |
| 6 | **Don't accept friend requests from strangers** – Fake profiles are often used for scams, phishing, or identity theft. |
| 7 | **Don't post anything you would regret** – The internet has a long memory. Deleted posts can still be screenshotted and shared. |
